Teguh Santoso Lembono

I am a PhD candidate at Ecole Polytechnique Federale de Lausanne (EPFL) and a researcher at Idiap Research Institute, Robot Learning and Interaction Group. I am currently involved in the Memory of Motion ( MEMMO ) project, the aim of which is to develop a generic locomotion controller for various legged robots using the combination of optimal control and machine learning.

I obtained a Bachelor of Engineering in Mechanical Engineering from Nanyang Technological University in 2012, and a Master of Science in Mechanical Engineering from National University of Singapore in 2016.

My research interests include optimization, machine learning, optimal control, and motion planning for robotics. In robotics, optimization is increasingly used to solve various problems including motion planning and control. As the robotics problem is often very difficult due to the non-linearity, good initialization becomes crucial. In my research, I investigate various methods to provide such good initial guesses. I work with function approximators such as GPR, GMR, and Multilayer Perceptron to learn the initialization from the data. I also consider probabilistic modelling techniques such as Generative Adversarial Network, Variational Inference, and Tensor methods to learn the probability distribution associated to the optimization cost function. I have worked on motion planning and control of dual arm robots (PR2), humanoid robot (Talos), quadruped (Solo, Anymal), and serial manipulator (UR, Denso, Panda).
